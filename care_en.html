Coramine (Nikethamide)

Coramine, also known as nikethamide, is a pharmaceutical drug that was historically used as a respiratory stimulant. It was intended to stimulate the central nervous system, particularly the respiratory center in the medulla oblongata.

History and Usage

In the mid-20th century, Coramine was widely used to treat conditions associated with respiratory depression, such as drug overdose, asphyxia, and shock. Over time, its use declined due to questionable efficacy and potential risks.

Mechanism of Action

The drug worked by stimulating the central nervous system, increasing the activity of the respiratory and vasomotor centers. This led to increased breathing rate and elevated blood pressure.

Modern Perspective

Today, Coramine is virtually obsolete in medical practice due to insufficient evidence of its effectiveness and potential side effects. Safer and more effective alternatives are available for treating respiratory depression and other conditions.

Important Information

When considering any medication for the treatment or alleviation of symptoms related to Gilbert Syndrome, it is crucial to consult a qualified healthcare professional. Self-medication can lead to undesirable consequences and complications.

Isha Kriya: A Simple Meditation for Well-being

Isha Kriya is a simple yet powerful meditation designed to enhance health and well-being. It was introduced by Sadhguru, a yogi and spiritual teacher, founder of the Isha Foundation.

What is Isha Kriya?

Isha Kriya is intended to help individuals reduce stress, improve mental clarity, and promote overall physical and emotional health. The practice takes about 12â€“18 minutes and can be performed at any convenient time.

Benefits for People with Gilbert Syndrome

While Isha Kriya is not a medical treatment, meditation can be beneficial for individuals with Gilbert Syndrome by helping to reduce stress and improve overall well-being. Stress can impact symptom manifestation, and managing stress may enhance quality of life.

How to Practice Isha Kriya

The practice is simple and requires no prior experience in meditation:

  1. Sit in a comfortable posture with your spine erect, hands resting on your thighs, palms facing upward.
  2. Close your eyes and direct your attention inward.
  3. Follow the guided meditation, which includes breathing techniques and a mantra.

You can find detailed instructions and free guided sessions on the official Isha Foundation website or through their app.

Important Information

Isha Kriya meditation is a complement to standard care and does not replace professional medical advice. Always consult your physician regarding the treatment of Gilbert Syndrome.

Nada Yoga by Sadhguru

Nada Yoga, or the yoga of sound, is a practice that utilizes sounds and vibrations to achieve inner harmony and joy. Sadhguru explains that uttering certain sounds can create an inner atmosphere of joy, making it a natural way to be.

Specifically, chanting the sound "AUM" (OM) is considered a fundamental practice in yoga, laying the foundation for more complex practices. This simple technique is suitable for beginners and can be performed anywhere: at home, in the office, or outdoors.

Sadhguru offers a 10-minute guided Nada Yoga practice that helps balance the body and mind, reducing anxiety, fear, and other negative states. This practice also aids in strengthening the immune system and improving concentration.
